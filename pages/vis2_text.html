What did you learn from the Twitterverse?

Human Impact

Over 13 years, the topic "Importance of Human Intervention" on Twitter saw an increase from one tweet in 2006 to half a million in 2019, indicating a rising interest in human impact on climate. The stance evolved from neutral to predominantly 'believer' in 2019, while sentiment remained fairly neutral, shifting marginally from 0.09 to 0.04, and the discussions decreased over time in aggressiveness.

Over 13 years, Twitter conversations about gas emissions have evolved from one tweet in 2006 to 73,000 in 2019, reflecting a growing awareness of their seriousness. Initially characterized by negative sentiment and aggressiveness, these discussions, though less frequent, now predominantly show a 'believer' stance in climate change, with a shift towards a neutral sentiment and decreasing aggressiveness.

For the topic "Ideological Positions of Global Warming" on Twitter, discussions grew from just two tweets in 2006 to 58,000 in 2019, reflecting escalating interest. The stance consistently aligned with 'believers' in global warming, paired with a negative sentiment. Over time, there was a noticeable increase in the aggressiveness of these discussions as number of tweets grew, highlighting a more intense engagement with the subject.

For "Weather Extremes", discussions expanded from 7 out of 12 tweets between 2006 and 2019. Initially neutral, the stance gradually shifted towards 'believer', while sentiment transitioned from neutral to negative. Interestingly, the level of aggressiveness in these discussions decreased over time.
Regarding "Significance of Pollution Awareness Events", there were no records in 2006, but by 2019, it garnered 125,000 tweets. This topic displayed no aggressiveness, with sentiment evolving from positive in 2007 to negative by 2019. Moreover, the stance underwent a shift from 'believer' to 'neutralist' during this period.
