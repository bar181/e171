The Climate Change Twitter Dataset is the most comprehensive dataset to date on climate change and human opinions on Twitter, featuring over 15 million tweets from a 13-year span. It includes detailed information such as topic categorization, stance and sentiment on climate change, and aggressiveness. Topic Categorization: The dataset categorizes tweets into specific topics related to climate change, enabling a focused analysis of public discourse on various aspects of the issue. Stance on Climate Change: It identifies the most popular stance on climate change for each topic and year, revealing public opinion trends over time. Sentiment Analysis: The dataset provides the average sentiment of tweets per topic and year, reflecting the general tone and mood of public discussions on climate change. Aggressiveness of Tweets: It measures the level of aggressiveness in tweets, offering insights into the intensity and nature of climate change debates on social media.
